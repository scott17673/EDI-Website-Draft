Who We Work With

Industries

We work across all industries. These are just the most common ones.

Food & Beverage

ED Industrial is part of the PROBAT Group, world leaders in food processing machinery. We already work with some of the highest volume food plants in the GTA including coffee, sauce, and bakery operations that run around the clock. Conveyors and drives, machine modifications, process piping, custom guarding and platforms. If your facility needs a mechanical contractor that knows food processing, we are already in it.

Concrete

Batch plant screw conveyors, weigh hoppers, mixer drive assemblies, and aggregate handling systems need consistent attention or production stops. Precast facilities add overhead crane maintenance, casting bed alignment, and form vibrator servicing to the list.

Metal Refineries

Overhead cranes run hard and the window to inspect them is short. Furnace drive systems, cooling water pumps, and process conveyors all degrade faster in high heat and corrosive conditions. Bearings, seals, and couplings wear in ways that aren't always obvious until something goes down.

Mining & Aggregate

Jaw and cone crushers need liner changes and eccentric bearing work on a real schedule. Screen decks wear through media and side plates. Conveyor systems need belt tracking, idler replacements, and drive pulley re-lagging. Kilns add tire and trunnion condition, gear and pinion wear, and shell alignment to the list.

Asphalt Plants

Drum dryer flights wear down, trunnions and tires need monitoring, and drag conveyors lose chain tension and chew through sprockets on a shorter cycle than most plants expect. Hot oil systems need pump seals and valve packing before every season.

Building Products

Extruders, augers, hydraulic brick presses, and block forming equipment run hard and the wear patterns are consistent if you know what to look for. Kiln car systems, setting machines, and off-kiln conveyors all have failure points that shut down output fast. Head pulleys and drive components on grinding room conveyors wear predictably but get ignored until they don't. Tivar lining on hoppers and chutes goes quietly until material starts hanging up and backing into the process.
