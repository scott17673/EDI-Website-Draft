Hamilton's Industrial Landscape

Manufacturing in Hamilton, Ontario

Hamilton is heavy industry. The city's industrial identity was built on steel. Stelco and Dofasco shaped the north end, and that legacy continues with ArcelorMittal Dofasco operating one of North America's largest steelmaking complexes along Burlington Street East. Beyond steel, Hamilton's industrial base includes recycling and waste processing along Parkdale Avenue and Gage Avenue, food and beverage manufacturing in the east end, chemical processing, and heavy equipment manufacturing.

The Bayfront industrial area, the Sherman Avenue corridor, and the Stoney Creek industrial parks all run active manufacturing operations. Hamilton plants tend to be older, heavier, and harder on equipment. The kind of environment where mechanical contractors earn their keep.
